Closed plant terraria


Small scale self sustaining plant ecosystems.

About

Living plant terraria made partly with different mosses collected from Danish forests, partly household plants that love moisture.

These terraria are a beautiful item to any household that enjoys having plants, and they require a minimum amount of maintenance.

The life in the terraria does not only include plants and mosses, but springtails are also added in order to fight mold.

False bottom

Every terrarium comes with a false bottom consisting of small rocks, charcoal and a filter.

Soil

A mixture of potting soil, orchid mixture soil and cocoa fiber is ideal for a closed terrarium.

Plants and mosses

Plants and mosses are placed in the terraria to make beautiful landscapes.

Maintenance

This comes as a surprise for many: Terraria have to be maintained. Some plants grow faster than others, meaning that some species will fill the space in the container faster than others, overtaking in time the whole container. Unless one wants a terrarium with only one species of plant, one needs to get in there and cut back the length of some plants.

Trimming back the leaves that are considered too large can become a big job, therefore patience is necessary.

Watering

When opening the lid of a closed plant terrarium, water evaporates. The evaporated water needs therefore to be replaced. Spray the glass of the container using a spray bottle. Careful though, not to add too much water. The soil should be moist, but not soggy. Rain water or distilled water is recomended. When watering with tap water, white streaks to appear on the glass due to mineral deposits.

If however too much water was added, then leave the lid open for a few days, until excess water evaporates again.

Condensation

It is normal to see condensation in the morning and evening. That means that the system works, and the water will soon fall on the bottom again.

If the container appears to be foggy, then there is too much water in the container. Leave the jar open for a while.

Placement

The terrarium should be kept in a bright place, but not in direct sunlight. The container's glass acts like a magnifier, therefore it will get too hot inside the container and create a sauna effect.

The terrarium should not be placed near a heat source, like a radiator, a TV etc..

If plants are seen to lean on one side, it means that they are leaning towards the light source. Turn the terrarium from time to time, or try to find a brighter place for the terrarium.

Finishing touches

When all is done, and you are geting ready to close the lid again, remember to dry the excess water from the walls of the container. Cleaning the glass of the terrarium will also help the light to reach the plants.
