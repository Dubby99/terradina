Closed plant terraria


Small scale self sustaining plant ecosystems.

About

Hobby company that creates living plant terraria made partly with different mosses collected from Danish forests, partly household plants that love moisture.

These terraria are a beautiful item to any household that enjoys having plants, and they require a minimum amount of maintenance.

What is planted in the terrarium depends largely on what can be found on the market in the season.

The life in the terraria does not only include plants and mosses, but springtails are also added in order to fight mold.

Layering


A proper drainage and soil will maintain the health of the plants and critters in the terrarium. Terrariums from Terradina come with different layers and soil, each having a different purpose.

False bottom

Every terrarium comes with a false bottom consisting of small rocks, charcoal and a filter.

Soil

A mixture of potting soil, orchid mixture soil and coconut fiber is ideal for a closed terrarium.

Plants and mosses

Plants and mosses are placed in the terraria to make beautiful landscapes.

Drainage layer

Starting from the bottom, the very first layer is a drainage layer. This one is very important because it helps the soil not to be too wet and soggy. The excess water will run off through the soil and into the drainage layer at the bottom.

This layer is vital for the terrarium to survive and thrive. Not having this layer can lead to plant root rot, meaning that the plant will be damaged and eventually die.

Activated charcoal layer

The second layer consists of charcoal. With the lid always on, the closed terrarium will produce odors from the activities in the terrarium: decomposing soil, mold etc..

Screen and moss layer

The third layer in the terrariums from Terradina consists of a screen and moss layer. This acts as a barrier so that the soil will not fall into the drainage and charcoal layer.

The screen is placed between the charcoal layer and the sphagnum moss. The moss is expected to decompose in time and fall into the false bottom. The screen will help to prevent this.

The moss layer will cover and hide the screen entirely, giving the terrarium a more natural look, while holding the soil in its place and providing moisture.

Soil layer

Finally, the last part is the soil layer. A mixture of different kinds of soils and coconut fiber will keep from getting the soil too compacted.

What to expect


Owning a terrarium is generally easy. However, one can be unlucky. Like any other plant arrangement, moving the terrarium in can be fatal if one misses some small details, like its placement in the new home and overwatering.

It is important to keep an eye on its development from time to time, especially in the first few weeks. This will pay off in the long run.

You may open the lid - this is actually recommended to do at least once a month. However, many had success with growing plants in a completely sealed closed terrarium for long periods of time.

Maintenance

This comes as a surprise for many: Terraria have to be maintained. Some plants grow faster than others, meaning that some species will fill the space in the container faster than others, overtaking in time the whole container. Unless one wants a terrarium with only one species of plant, one needs to get in there and cut back the length of some plants.

Trimming back the leaves that are considered too large can become a big job, therefore patience is necessary.

Watering

When opening the lid of a closed plant terrarium, water evaporates. The evaporated water needs therefore to be replaced. Spray the glass of the container using a spray bottle. Careful though, not to add too much water. The soil should be moist, but not soggy. Rain water or distilled water is recomended. When watering with tap water, white streaks to appear on the glass due to mineral deposits.

If however too much water was added, then leave the lid open for a few days, until excess water evaporates again.

Condensation

It is normal to see condensation in the morning and evening. That means that the system works, and the water will soon fall on the bottom again. Both the plants and soil in the terrarium release moisture and water vapor. This condenses against the walls of the terrarium and falls back into the soil to be used again.

If the container appears to be foggy all the time, then there is too much water in the container. Leave the jar open for a while.

Placement

The terrarium should be kept in a bright place, but not in direct sunlight. The container's glass acts like a magnifier, therefore it will get too hot inside the container and create a sauna effect.

The terrarium should not be placed near a heat source, like a radiator, a TV etc..

If plants are seen to lean on one side, it means that they are leaning towards the light source. Turn the terrarium from time to time, or try to find a brighter place for the terrarium.

Finishing touches

When all is done, and you are getting ready to close the lid again, remember to dry the excess water from the walls of the container. Cleaning the glass of the terrarium will also help the light to reach the plants.
